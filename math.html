Truncated Pyramid Formulas

1. Volume of a Truncated Pyramid

The volume of a truncated pyramid can be calculated using the following formula:

Formula: \( V = \frac{1}{3} \times h \times (A_1 + A_2 + \sqrt{A_1 \times A_2}) \)

Where:

  • h = Vertical height of the truncated pyramid (distance between the two parallel bases)
  • A₁ = Area of the lower base
  • A₂ = Area of the upper base

Steps to Calculate the Volume

  • Step 1: Measure the areas of the upper and lower bases, A₁ and A₂.
  • Step 2: Measure the vertical height between the two bases, h.
  • Step 3: Add the areas of the two bases: \( A_1 + A_2 \).
  • Step 4: Multiply the height by the sum of the areas and the square root of their product: \[ h \times (A_1 + A_2 + \sqrt{A_1 \times A_2}) \]
  • Step 5: Divide the result by 3 to get the volume: \[ V = \frac{1}{3} \times h \times (A_1 + A_2 + \sqrt{A_1 \times A_2}) \]

2. Surface Area of a Truncated Pyramid

The surface area of a truncated pyramid is the sum of the areas of both bases and the lateral area. The formula is:

Formula: \( A = A_1 + A_2 + \text{Lateral Area} \)

The lateral area is calculated using:

\[ \text{Lateral Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times (P_1 + P_2) \times l \]

Where:

  • A₁ = Area of the lower base
  • A₂ = Area of the upper base
  • P₁ = Perimeter of the lower base
  • P₂ = Perimeter of the upper base
  • l = Slant height of the truncated pyramid

3. Slant Height of a Truncated Pyramid (using trigonometry)

The slant height of a truncated pyramid can be calculated using trigonometry if you know the height and the difference between the base widths (if rectangular) or diameters (if circular):

Formula: \( l = \sqrt{h^2 + \left(\frac{d_1 - d_2}{2}\right)^2} \)

Where:

  • h = Vertical height of the truncated pyramid
  • d₁ = Diameter or width of the lower base
  • d₂ = Diameter or width of the upper base

4. Height of a Truncated Pyramid (using trigonometry)

If the slant height and the widths of the bases are known, the vertical height can be calculated as:

Formula: \( h = \sqrt{l^2 - \left(\frac{d_1 - d_2}{2}\right)^2} \)
